The hold-out is located in the Houston Fifth Ward. Unfortunately, there are a lot of problems existing in this area such as high crime rate, no access to fresh food, abandoned houses and vacant blocks.

above, hold out analysis diagram

The highlighted area above is the “hold-out” in context. The hold-out had no defining characteristics, there was an even distribution of housing, vacant areas, civic and commercial.Calculating the total continuity of the region, taking consideration of all intersections (four-way, three-way, two-way, one-way, and missing intersections), the continuity comes to around 78.6% for the average of the area.

The design proposal for this project is comprised of two steps: firstly developing a clearing strategy for the areas around the hold-out (hold-out is untouched in this project), and secondly designing a reconstructing system that can be implemented easily to transform the surroundings to eco-friendly and high-efficient communities.

above, second level plan and module section

The system proposed for clearing and reconstructing is comprised of three scales: the urban scale, the block scale and the human scale.

A pixelized approach is firstly applied to clear the surrounding blocks around the hold-out. Then cruciforms are introduced to anchor the pixelized field and provide centers to the area. Finally, a green path way is added to connect all the cruciforms and housing modules are designed to enrich this system.

above, upa studies

While the darker black represents a 48-story highrise, the lighter grey represents a midrise of 16 floors. Four buildings can be organized into one block to maximize the green coverage. Clusters are formed to anchor the pixels because of the strong form that brings regularity to the irregularity of the pixels. The goal for this project was to achieve a UPA between 80-120, and the diagrammatic studies shows that a 100 UPA gives more varieties and complexities.

above, courtyard diagram

Wood is the primary material used in this project for both the structure and the facade. Wood louver patterns create a harmonious exterior wall system, provide shading for the interior and define outdoor spaces. The use of wood as the main material not only saves the budget but also helps carbon storage and urban sequestration.

above, site plan diagram

The buildings are laid out across the site orthogonally to continue the original city grid to the north of the, which at the same time brings people efficient circulation and shortcuts. The layout also implies a diagonal movement across the site, which refers to the second city grid to the south of the site and provide people meandering experiences.
